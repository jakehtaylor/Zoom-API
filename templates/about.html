Now, more than ever, meetings and conferences are happening virtually, primarily hosted by applications like Zoom. It is more than likely that this trend will continue to pick up steam, and it will become crucial for businesses to harness the advantages of hosting events on these virtual platforms, but most businesses aren't equipped to do this.

Zoom provides complete activity logs for every meeting, however, these logs are difficult to understand and contain large amounts of unnecessary information.

This app exhibits several ways to turn these dense logs into meaningful analytics for company meetings and large conferences alike, with no coding experience required.

Accessing the Logs

  1. Sign in to the Zoom web portal.
  2. In the navigation panel, click Dashboard.
  3. At the top of the Dashboard screen, click the Meetings tab.
  4. Click Past Meetings.
  5. Click the desired Meeting ID number, then click Export details to CSV.
  6. Once the report has been processed, click Downloads at the top of the page.
  7. Click Meetings to access the generated reports.
  8. Click Download to download and view the report.

Using Zoom Analytics

  1. Save the .csv formatted logs to a new folder on your computer.
  2. Navigate to the app's cleaning page, either through the link at the top right of each page, or the blue button under the file upload on the landing page.
  3. Upload* the log file and click Auto-clean
  4. The app will run a Levenshtein distance algorithm to check for users who might have used slightly different names over the course of the meeting, and a simpler check for names that are abnormally formatted. This might take a few seconds.
  5. You'll be taken to a page to review and select every potential change discovered by the App. Follow the instructions and Click the Submit button at the bottom of the page once you've selected and inputted all of your desired changes.
  6. You'll be redirected to a page with a link to download a .json data file that contains the information the program needs to make all of your selected substitutions. Save this file into the same folder that contains your zoom logs.
  7. Return to the landing page and Click Choose Files.
  8. In the pop-up file selection window, navigate into the folder housing your .csv zoom logs and the .json cleaning instructions, and select both files. Click Open to upload* the two files.
  9. Click Generate Metrics.

*This application does not save any user-uploaded files into server storage. When you upload a file, the file is read for the necessary information and lost upon redirection.
